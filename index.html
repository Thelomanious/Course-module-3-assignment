The dish is called Golden Sesame Chicken Stir-Fry. It starts with fresh, tender chicken breast slices. The chicken is marinated in a light soy sauce. Fresh ginger is finely grated into the marinade. Garlic is crushed to release its aromatic flavor. A dash of rice wine vinegar adds subtle tang. Sesame oil infuses the chicken with nutty aroma. A touch of honey provides a delicate sweetness. The chicken rests in the marinade for thirty minutes. Meanwhile, fresh vegetables are prepared. Red and yellow bell peppers are sliced thin. Crisp broccoli florets are washed and blanched. Carrots are cut into thin matchsticks. Snow peas are trimmed for tenderness. Green onions are sliced on the diagonal. Mushrooms are cleaned and halved. The wok is heated until smoking hot. A teaspoon of sesame oil is added to the pan. The marinated chicken is tossed into the wok. It sizzles as it hits the hot surface. The chicken is stirred constantly to brown evenly. The aroma fills the kitchen immediately. Once nearly cooked, the chicken is removed. More sesame oil is added for the vegetables. Carrots go in first, as they take the longest to cook. Broccoli is added next, keeping it bright green. Bell peppers follow for color and sweetness. Snow peas are added last to stay crisp. Sliced mushrooms are tossed gently into the mix. Garlic and ginger are added for extra fragrance. The vegetables are stir-fried over high heat. A little soy sauce is drizzled for seasoning. A splash of chicken stock is added for moisture. Cornstarch slurry thickens the sauce lightly. The chicken is returned to the wok. Everything is tossed together carefully. The sauce clings to both chicken and vegetables. Toasted sesame seeds are sprinkled over the top. Chopped cilantro adds fresh green color. A squeeze of lime juice brightens the flavors. The dish is plated over steaming jasmine rice.
